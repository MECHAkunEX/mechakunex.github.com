Game: Darkwing Duck (NES, 1992)20130325
I played this game for about forty minutes and beat the first
three levels. They had a degree of brutality that I just don't
enjoy.

In many ways the game is reminiscent of Mega Man, except you don't
gain abilities from bosses and the power-ups aren't useful
(they're like Castlevania power-ups in that they give you the
ability to shoot in different directions) and the consequences for
getting hurt seem much much higher. Apparently Darkwing's pain
threshold just isn't very high.

Every screen was a huge battle that wore me down. I decided to
leave it at that. I now know that this game is pretty solid, but
not better than Mega Man. The main distinctive thing about is the
large number of jumping/hanging platform sequences, which we
pretty neat.

Game: Little Samson (NES, 1992)20130325
I played this game for about forty minutes. I got through the
opening stages and then the next few levels. I fought some sort
of water monster and then fell into the ground and got my
partners taken away from me. Then I had to get through a stage
sitting atop a giant crab, which I was not able to do after quite
a few continues. I found that in the three boss battles I had,
the enemies took absurd amounts of damage. I got passed the first
one by using each of the characters in a sequence, using up all
their energy before going to the next one.

I feel like this game is really tight and nice for its age. I
don't have any connection to it and some things about it are very
frustrating and unsatisfying. I think I will put it back on the
queue as a "revisit later" game. The multiple characters are
definitely interesting, although it is a bit much to manage all
their energy gauges and then make sure you deliberately switch
away from one as it is about to die. Definitely a really
intriguing game, but not something I fell in love with right away.

Game: Shatterhand (NES, 1991)20130325
I played this for about twenty minutes and beat the first two
levels but died on the third after meeting an wall of resistance
in the form of lots of little monsters that swarmed me.

This felt like a solid, average, platform-action game with melee
combat. The only interesting things were the power-up system
based on three-letter strings of A and B, but all the power-ups
felt pretty useless so it wasn't worth learning which I should be
building up to. The other interesting thing was the way you had
to spend money on upgrades during the levels, but it was a bit
annoying because you didn't keep them from screen transition to
screen transition.

The two bosses I fought were really easy. I played each twice and
beat it without getting hit a single time on the second try,
because the pattern was so clear.

I can imagine having spent a lot of time on this game in an era
when there was nothing else to do, because it feels pretty
solid. But, today it doesn't feel strong enough to be worth an
investment.

Movie: Battle: Los Angeles, starring Aaron Eckhart (2011)20130324
I really liked this movie. I think my favourite part of it is that
(a) the war is defensive, (b) the idea that the military simpler
exists to protect civilians is very prominent, (c) the soldiers
are "everymen" who aren't the focal point of the war... except a
little bit at the end, and (d) the nobility of the troops. 

It's a very exciting movie too and the alien threat seems "real"
and not super comic-y. It was really great. I really liked the
depiction of aliens and the way the achieved superiority---the
cyborg enhancements and the way they were driving around
artillery/cannon like units.

Movie: The Adventures of Tintin, starring Jamie Bell (2011)20130320

found the style to be a bit strange... the heads on all the
characters except for Tintin are too big. It was definitely
striking visually.

Game: Adventures of Lolo 3 (NES, 1991)20130319
I started this and played up to Level 3 Room 9. The main
difference between this and the previous games is (a) the ability
to play as Lala, which seems to just be a sprite change, and (b)
the separate areas that divide up the puzzles into a kind of
narrative. I find it a bit stupid and tedious to have to do
things other than solve puzzles. It seems strange to me that the
creators would think that people really care about the "world" of
Lolo enough to want to feel like they are "exploring" it, rather
than just doing puzzles. In any case, the puzzles I've done so
far have been fun and have included a few tough ones. I'm looking
forward to completing it.
