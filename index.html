Book: Nutrition and Physical Degeneration, by Weston A. Price (1939) (3)20130507
I love that he says "oldsters".

The first chapter was very uninformative as it rehearsed old
rhetoric and statistics that are clearly no longer relevant.

Although his travel details in chapter two are cute, it is the
classic example of "what I did last summer" writing that is a sign
of an amateur scientist.

His main point seems to be that these Swiss kids work very hard and
eat primarily bread, milk, and cheese with meat on occasion. In
contrast, the other Swiss kids had "modern" diets of refined
flours, sweets, canned goods, sweetened fruits, chocolate and
reduced use of dairy products. At this point his focus is on their
worse teeth and susceptibility to tuberculosis.

Book: Nutrition and Physical Degeneration, by Weston A. Price (1939)20130504
My wife requested that I read this and write a summary for her
because she didn't want to read it herself.

I read up past the Introduction today, which means I just read the
rhetorical preview of what is covered without reading any of the
details. When I was vegan, I read lots of stuff like this about
how wonderful primitive diets were, especially with regards to
teeth. I'm interested in learning the details.

The introduction was very funny with its discussion of how the
bones in the head were affected by nutrition and in turn affected
brain development. And its discussion of "delinquents".
