Movie: The Adventures of Tintin, starring Jamie Bell (2011)20130320
My wife and I watched this without really knowing anything about
the story or what to expect. I had assumed it would be a "kids
movie", but it really wasn't designed for very young kids. I
thought that the plot was interesting and fairly advanced for the
overall feel of the movie. It seems superfluously
computer-animated. There's nothing about the world or the story
that really needs it, and instead because it is animated there are
some ridiculous set pieces that don't feel right for the world. I
found the style to be a bit strange... the heads on all the
characters except for Tintin are too big. It was definitely
striking visually.

Game: Adventures of Lolo 3 (NES, 1991)20130319
I started this and played up to Level 3 Room 9. The main
difference between this and the previous games is (a) the ability
to play as Lala, which seems to just be a sprite change, and (b)

narrative. I find it a bit stupid and tedious to have to do

creators would think that people really care about the "world" of
Lolo enough to want to feel like they are "exploring" it, rather
than just doing puzzles. In any case, the puzzles I've done so
far have been fun and have included a few tough ones. I'm looking
forward to completing it.

Game: Downtown Special: Kunio-kun no Jidaigeki da yo - Zenin Shūgō! (NES, 1991)20130319
As a concept, this game sounds amazing: River City Ransom with a
Japanese country-side theme and old-timey era. However, I found it
to be much less satisfying than River City Ransom. The game world
seems huge in comparison and confusingly organized: the majority
of the time I played I just wandered around in a big circle and
ended back in the starting town. I had no idea where I was
supposed to go or how to trigger the boss encounters that control
the progression of these games. The lack of context and the
increased world size really did damage to my appreciation of
it. The game did seem more complex in other ways too though: you
seem to be able to pick up, spin, and throw enemies, which was
new. Also, your "brother" is always present, if A.I. controlled,
which is neat. Nevertheless, I couldn't play it and put it down.

Game: Ni no Kuni: Wrath of the White Witch (PS3, 2013) (9)20130318
I played today for two and a half hours and beat the game. I
basically just had to go through the final dungeon and beat the
final boss marathon. I didn't find the bosses that challenging.
The formula was to use the last two spells over and over and then
use items to get back some MP. It was a bit of a disappointing
ending. In addition, there's not really a final cut-scene... they
spent most of their "ending" juice on the faux-ending is seems.

Overall, I'd say that this game was beautiful and mechanically
sound, but too boring for my taste. There was a lot of variability
in the game... like the treat system and the huge number of
familiars... that I never really needed to use, which felt like a
let down. I wish there would have been more reason to switch
familiars, for example, rather than just find a few that I had
affinities for and use them the whole time. I also wish that
faster modes of ground transportation would have unlocked, because
moving around in the game started to feel really tedious.

I can imagine recommending the game to a kid, but I don't think I
would to an adult and definitely not a marginal RPG fan. If you
are just going to play one or two RPGs, there are much better
options.
