Game: Rocket Knight Adventures (GEN, 1993)20130811
I played this game today and beat it on Hard (with Save States at
each checkpoint, so basically infinite lives). It was pretty
brutal. In Hard, not only do you only start with One Life, but
everything kills you on one hit, so it's like playing a shmup and
doing a 1CC.

I found the general mechanics to be pretty much just okay. Not
terrible, but not spectacular either. However, the "set pieces"
and sequences in the game that were different we really cool. The
"puzzle rooms" in Stage 5 and the forced scrolling chase were
really neat. Stage 6, which was entirely in the shooter style, was
very fun too. It was awesome to take down the ship piece by piece.

It was also cool how even after fighting the final boss, you still
had to dodge and weave as it chased you to Earth. It was a good
tough.

In general, the graphics were some of the best I've seen on the
Genesis and I was genuinely impressed. 

Nevertheless, it feels like a one-off game that I couldn't get a
lot from another time.

Movie: The Host, based on the work by Stephanie Meyer (2013)20130808
I was generally skeptical about whether this movie would be any
good. I found a lot of the acting a little "eh", but the world was
really cool. It's like a Sci-Fi version of the *REDACTED* doctrine that
Satan (and his followers) have no body, but want one, and can
possess humans and that their goal is for everyone to be "good" but
without the agency to decide to be good. A lot of what goes on in
the movie is clearly deeply inspired by this idea.

Some of the worst parts of the movie are classic Stephanie though:
for instance, the crazy love square with two people in one body,
which is at its craziest when Wanderer kisses two boys within
minutes.

There are some logical problems with the plot that I thought of,
or at least big questions:

- How do the Souls originally start the invasion? We see them enter
bodies by making an incision and then crawling in. It seems like
to start off, they would have to arrive not in "Soul" form but in
other bodies on traditional ships. Yet, we see them only travel
through space in their little Soul capsules.

- If it takes so long to travel between planets, as Wanderer says,
then how is it that there are Souls arriving periodically in the
first place. They would have had to have left long long before
the invasion of Earth started, confident that they would
eventually take it over, and given that the Souls do not appear
to be conscious during their capsule time, how would they steer
to the planet unless they knew already that it was inhabitable
and knew where the landing pad would be?

I like it when a book or movie leaves lots of interesting questions
about its universe. I'm interested in reading the book now.

Game: Avenging Spirit (GB, 1992)20130807

Game: Landstalker (GEN, 1992)20130807
I was incredibly excited about this game. I've heard Tim Rogers,
whom I adore, talk about how great it is. I played Alundra and
remember it fondly.

But... man, was I let down!

I find it totally impossible to control. It makes no sense. In the
opening sequence, I was standing on top of a platform and when I
pressed Up it did the same thing as pressing Right (and similarly
with Left and Down.) How was I supposed to get the Treasure Chest
unless I do more than just go on one particular track? It was
infuriating. It seems like the only way to move is press two
buttons at the same time, but it was almost impossible to move
this way with any finesse and predict where you will end up.

I just imagined having to go through difficult platforming
sequences like they show in the opening and it was enough to
totally turn me away. Ick! I can't believe something like this
could ever exist!

Also, it was really weird to get two life stocks before even
getting in one fight. What's the point of starting with 4 if you
immediately get 6 without needing to do anything?

I can imagine that someone might like this game, but I can't see
how.
