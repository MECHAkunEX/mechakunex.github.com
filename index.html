it to the other planet, but haven't talked to anyone yet. I
thought the movie as the characters were launching to the other
planet was pretty awesome... particularly Fourier's army. I like
how much mystery there is around the other planet, the ring around
the planet the characters are from, etc. Although, I dislike how
little the characters seem to be reacting to the idea that there
is another planet, more people, etc.

It's been very tough to not have Sophie in the party. I've
decided to drop down the difficulty level to Evil, since no one
else can heal as effectively as she can. This is a little tough
for me, because (something I forgot to mention I liked yesterday)
the higher difficulties give you benefits. (As opposed to a game
like Persona, where the higher difficulty levels just punish you
and for you to grind more.)

Exploring the Snowshroud Ruins was a little ridiculous because all
the switches had the worst initial placement. It was almost absurd
how predictable it was that they'd be in the wrong place.

In Telos Astue, I noticed a typo in a skit subtitling: it said
"my" rather than "may" when Hubert was speaking.

Game: Tales of Graces f (PS3, 2010) (13)20130119
This morning I played for about three hours and made it through
Fendel Tower. After my experience on the mountain, I had enough
strength to totally cream most enemies today---including the Lab
boss on Chaos! I'm starting to run out of titles to acquire and
I'm starting to master old ones. I really enjoy the naming
convention on the Amarcians: Fermat, Pascal, Fourier, Gauss. :)

Later in the day, I played for another three hours and go to
before going to World's Eye. Although I'm a bit confused, because
I forgot there was a port in Zhavert... although I guess that's
how I got there? I had a very tough time with the enemies on
Chaos in the ice cave. I also decided to specifically try to get
my characters to use more attacks to gain Adept and Master titles
for them.

After my kids went to bed, I played for another hour and half. I
got to where the Shuttle is, but haven't taken the detour to
Lhant yet. I was very proud of my fight against Richard, because
I was able to beat him on Evil. (I got creamed on Chaos.) I
progressed the story a bit and went on the shipwreck side-quest
in Oul Raye, but died because of stupid Hubert. It's really
annoying to not have Sophie in my party, because she's an
essential part of my strategy and I've been using her the whole
time. This sequence of the game is a bit annoying in the amount
of running around you need to do. It feels like I spend more time
walking to a place than being there are fighting/learning about
the story.

After such a long day playing, I reflected a bit on the game. I
really like the story as an avenue for the characters to
interact. I think my favourite character is Pascal. They really
sell the way she acts and I like the way they don't have to have
pseudo-science explanations, because she just uses terms like
"thingamajigger" and such. It's cute.

I really enjoy the combat system. If I were playing on a
different difficulty level, I could imagine feeling it was boring
and rote, but because I have it at the maximum... it is pretty
stressful and requires a nice rhythm to it. I'm feeling a little
uncomfortable with it at the moment, because I'm trying to go
outside my comfort zone to get different titles for Asbel, but
it's still good. It's pretty awesome.

I played throughout the morning today. I got to the Amarcian
Enclave and verified my hypothesis that Pascal is really an
Amarcian and that there are few left. When we got there, it was
interesting that one of the people said that we were people from
"the world below"---meaning they are in the sky. I had previously
assumed that they were underground. I wonder if the weird ring you
sometimes see in the sky is one of their creations or something?

There were some funny moments today in the skits. I enjoyed one
where Sophie said her favourite food was Crablettes (Crab
Omelettes) and Malik couldn't get a word in edgewise about the
food he liked. There was another where Hubert said that Cheria
was dressed indecently and he couldn't believe how much of her
thigh was revealed in combat. It was very funny to have two react
to that situation. :)

Gameplay-wise, I unlocked the Chaos difficulty. It is incredibly
hard. Almost every random battle can take me out. I think I
should camp on Mt. Zhavert for a bit and get up some
levels. There was a time when I had to beat one enemy at a time
and then run back to the Save Point because I was so nervous
about dying and losing progress. (I died maybe... six times in
that area.) When I fought the Boar boss at the end, I did pretty
well on Chaos, but ultimately dropped it to Evil to do it.

I did the whole first set of the Shot Cube puzzle game. It's
pretty fun.
