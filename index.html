Book: Divergent, by Veronica Roth (2011) (3)20140728
I just read the chapter that reveals the story takes place in
Chicago (via the reference to the Bean.)

It is very hard to tell how many people exist in the Divergent
world. The fact that entire factions could meet together and the
leaders know the initiates suggests that it is very small
population.

The book is strange because it basically has no real plot. It's all
character development with a tiny bit of foreshadowing of a problem
between Erudite and Abnegation. The main point is the romance and
the challenge of becoming Dauntless. In the chapter I just read, it
seems clear that we're entering PLOT MODE where the war and action
will start (or at least be thwarted.)

--

The end is pretty cool.

I'd say this book is totally on par with The Hunger Games and Harry
Potter. The Potter world is definitely better because it has 7 huge
books to become huge. The Hunger Games is more action packed. But I
feel like this book is better written and is deeper.

Book: Divergent, by Veronica Roth (2011)20140723
The start of this book is interesting, but clearly it is yet
another instance of the Harry Potter/Twilight/Hunger Games
genre. This time, the dystopian world is made up of groups that
exemplify some virtue and disdain others. It's kind of an absurd
set up. 

Things like this (and the Houses of Harry Potter) don't really
exist in the real world because people are members of many
different complementary groups---their religion, their
neighborhood, their philosophy, etc---and since there isn't
necessarily an overlap, they aren't quite as cliquish. I think the
young are susceptible to cliques because they only care about a
small number of things so they form a solid boundary around that
thing. Some religious communities seem to want to create a single
group as well, but even *REDACTED*s don't effectively do it.

It's bizarre that being "divergent" would be "dangerous". It seems
like she would just not excel in whatever faction she goes
with. The other thing about the world is that the world doesn't
seem particularly dystopian... and the Choosing Ceremony seems to
establish that it is the past pre-Faction world that destroyed
things and the Factions are hanging on. Perhaps the plot will be
about the Factionless? Or maybe what lives beyond the city?
