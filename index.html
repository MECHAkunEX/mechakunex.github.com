Game: Dust: An Elysian Tail (XBLA, 2012)20130212
I started this game in the evening after getting through
Spelunky. It has been on my mind since I first saw a trailer for
it a few years before it came out. Brad on the Giantbombcast had a
lot of good things to say about it, so I was excited to try it out
eventually.

I played for a little more than two hours and my first impressions
of the game were good. The graphics are beautiful. The voice
acting is not horrific, but not amazing either so I'm tending to
just read the subtitles and skip to the next piece of text. The
world is very interesting and doesn't feel to "same-y".

I'm playing on Hardcore mode and the combat feels pretty tight
and fun. It's very comparable to Muramasa, except I feel like
this game doesn't have as much to offer and doesn't have any
complex/interesting mechanic like the sword strength
maintenance. I haven't found the Fidget attacks to be very
useful, so the game is really just attacking with the sword and
dodging for me. Since I'm on Hardcore, I basically cannot get hit
a single time or I will die. (I have 80 HP and most attacks do
about 120 HP damage right now.) This has the bonus of making it
so there is no point to using healing items because everything is
a one-hit kill anyways.

So far I've only seen four types of monsters and they are
basically all fodder if you pay a little bit of attention to
where they are and dodge properly.

The speed of the game is good when you are progressing into new
areas, but when you are going back to old areas it is very
painful because the movement is just too slow when you know where
you are going.

Game: Spelunky (XBLA, 2012) (4)20130212
I played today for about two and half hours. After less than an
hour, I had unlocked the Ice Caves short-cut. Within 15 minutes, I
had gotten to the Temple twice. I found the Ice Caves to be pretty
easy, but also really random. Since it is so vertical and open,
you can pretty easily jump right to the end. This means for me
that it isn't really worth it to open up the short-cut, although
it is painful to go through. After another thirty minutes or so, I
got to Olmec but didn't realize where his hit box is or something
and died right away. (I only had 1 HP anyways.) After another
thirty minutes or so I got back and did him in. I like that he's
basically Bowser from Super Mario Brothers 3.

Overall, I had 209 attempts. I know that there's another way to
beat the game (by going through all the secret areas) but I don't
love the game enough to do it. (That's why I marked it as not
completed.) Maybe I haven't played long enough to truly discover
it, but I feel like the game really doesn't have that much of
complex strategy. When I eventually won or got to Olmec, I wasn't
doing much different than the other times. There were lots of
really bizarre random chances. It's definitely fun, but not enough
fun to invest a ton more time into honing my skills. (On the other
hand, I've watched a few videos that show people dying and
starting again and they have amazing technique that is basically
perfect every time. So there's definitely something there, but I
don't want to achieve that level.)

I had a lot of fun with it and could imagine coming back to it if
I could have a tighter loop of getting into it. It would also be
awesome if the co-op were more effective for my situation.

Compared to Fez, which I just played, I think Spelunky is a
/better/ game but not a game that I love enough to beat it with
the absoluteness I did to Fez, which seems contradictory, but I
think makes sense if you think about it.

Game: Tales of Graces f (PS3, 2010) (32)20130207
I looked up the stats on my New Game+. I ended with Asbel at level
80, which is crazy to me, because I was roughly that level after
getting to Zhonecage (after almost 100 hours) the first time. I
had 518 encounters in the New Game+, the total battle time was 48
minutes, which means the average battle length was 5.5 seconds!
It's also pretty amazing to me that only 9% of my play time was
fighting this time, especially because I skipped every cut-scene,
had 2x movement speed, and was just running from boss to boss.

I've decided that I'm done with the game. The only things left
that I could do is (a) get all the Carta cards, which would be
super tedious; (b) get the Turtlez Togz from the Dark Turtlez,
which would be super tedious; (c) get the Eleth Mixer to 9999,
which even checking out what apparently is the fastest way to do
it, would be crazy; and, (d) get the title trophies for the men,
which would probably be painful because it mean having lots of
battles and managing what skills they used.

This was a very fun game. The battle system is great and the story
is a lot of fun. The world is cool too. I think my only complaints
about it are very minor: the user interface on some things could
have been better. For example, being able to have sets of AI
settings for different situations, sets of mixer settings as well,
and being able to pick a result you wanted from the dualizing
system (that you had already discovered) rather than having to
remember the recipe in your head. The AI was also pretty
unreliable as a healer. I found that it almost never tried to
resurrect dead characters and if you told it to, then it was hit,
it would not remember what your last instruction was. This was
very annoying.
