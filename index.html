Game: Shin Megami Tensei: Persona 4 Golden (Vita, 2012) (15)20130531
I played for about a month and a half of time in the game and did
a lot of social stuff and got to the next dungeon. I enjoyed the
conclusion of a lot of the social links. 

I like how clean and cute the relationships with the girls are. I
would be embarrassed (and probably not play) if they were risque,
but I love the innocence of how the culmination of the
relationship is a hug and declaration of love. 

One complaint I have about the social link system is that when
you make a girl your "girlfriend" and you already have one,
there's really no downside (as long as you've reached 10 with
your other one.) You're really just changing who you will be
involved with in special scenes like the festival date. I think
it would be better if the game tried to make you feel bad about
it with a breakup scene or something.

Book: Nutrition and Physical Degeneration, by Weston A. Price (1939) (19)20130527
I didn't really get anything out of Chapter 20 either. It focused
mostly on how many of the ills of our food lacking vitamins was due
to not taking care of the soil the way it was primitively.

Something that I've been thinking about recently with this book
(not specifically to do with Chapter 20) is that many of the ills
he observes in modern culture could be considered advantages. For
example, yes there is more tooth decay, but one interpretation is
that in primitive cultures if you have tooth decay, you just die
because you can't eat. Thus, the power of our technology (such as
medicine and the way we can produce food) covers the weakness of
our bodies---which is the normal role of technology. Similarly,
this "defends" the presence (or rather, survival) of those with
physical disorders.

Game: Final Fantasy 5 (SNES, 1992) (9)20130524
Today I played for about six hours (in two chunks) and beat the
game. I played from the Solitary Island Temple to the end of the
game.

It really is remarkable how this game is connected to Final
Fantasy 6. There's Gogo. The final boss is made of many pieces
sticking out in a weird way. A lot of the same summoned monsters
are secret bosses. It's very cool.

I was able to beat the last few bosses by spamming Bahamut while
on Haste2, with the other characters providing backup. Some of
them were very nerve-wracking. I think the most tense part, other
than the final battle (which I did in one try), was getting from
the Castle save spot to the Necrophobe save spot. The *REDACTED* Prince
boss was very hard.

I beat the game at just level 36, which seems very low. I wasn't
able to beat the optional bosses Omega or Shinryuu and I didn't
try to go find Phoenix. 

This is definitely an amazing game that fits within the Final
Fantasy canon for me. I think I will continue to play it far into
the future. Next time I think I'll try a different class
configuration though. For instance, I never used the Magic
Knight, Dragon Knight, or even Black magic.

I'm a bit surprised I was able to beat it in just 27 hours, with
basically no grinding after I mastered my classes and even before
then, I mainly did grinding for gold. There's a lot of power in
the job system.

Total time: 27:28
