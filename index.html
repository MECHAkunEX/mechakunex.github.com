Movie: Apocalypto, directed by Mel Gibson (2006)20130116
This was a really interesting movie. It's is practically without
dialogue for huge stretches and its setting is virtually without
compare. It feels incredibly realistic, although I don't really
have any way to verify that. I'm kind of amazed that it was made.

I think the most crazy part was when the lady is nearly drowning in
the pit and happens to give birth because the fall "shocked" her
into it. It was a little bit too crazy, but it worked in the story.

The movie was far more gory than it needed to be, but I think I
understand why Gibson designed it that way.

It's cool that at the core it is a "chase" movie.

Movie: The Grey, starring Liam Neeson (2012)20130116

Enclave and verified my hypothesis that Pascal is really an
Amarcian and that there are few left. When we got there, it was

Book: Apollo's Angels, by Jennifer Homans (2010) (11)20130111
When I was reading today about ballet in post-WWII Britain, I came
across the strange discussion of /The Invitation/, a 1960 ballet by
the Royal Ballet Touring Company that featured a re-enacted rape
scene on stage. There's a footnote about how ballet was technically
not under the purview of the censors. As an example:

"In August 1960, representatives from the Lord Chamberlin's
office attended a performance of /Les Ballets Africains/ to
determine if the production was ballet or theatre. If ballet,
bare breasts were permissible; if theatre, strictly
forbidden. The authorities agreed it was a ballet---and the
press had a field day with photos." (pg. 433)

How strange.

Game: Hot Shots Golf: World Invitational (Vita, 2012) (9)20130110
I decided to take this off my list because I haven't played it in
so long and when I think about playing it, it makes me mad
because it's so hard to know what a good strategy would be.

Part of me feels like the game requires you to understand
something about golf... because it doesn't give you any assistance
understanding what will happen because of the wind, ground
curvature, or grass length, etc---but requires you to figure it
out. I don't find that my intuition for that stuff is growing.

and I think that it is very well made, so I think deserves a high

Movie: Jiro Dreams of Sushi, by David Gelb (2011)20130109
I didn't have high expectations for this movie, because I didn't
really have expectations at all. After we watched it, I wanted to
save up to go to this place some day and eat sushi right now. It
was amazingly engrossing for such a strange subject. I enjoyed it
a lot and liked to see the little things that they thought made
their practice different. My favourite parts were probably...
- The octopus crawling up the dude.
- The apprentice talking about making 200 egg sushi that were
rejected over four months.
- The rice dealer talking about how he wouldn't sell his rice to
the Hyatt because they wouldn't be able to cook it.
