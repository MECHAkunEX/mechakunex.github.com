Game: Castlevania: Aria of Sorrow (GBA, 2003)

Oct 2nd, 2009

During the last little bit, I played and beat this for the Nintendo GBA. This is definitely a good entry in the Castlevania franchise. I really appreciated the variety that came from the Soul system, but I felt like it had a problem that many RPGs with these “collecting” systems: most of the things you collect are totally useless. This game had two other problems in my mind: first, the difficulty curve had a really sharp spike at one point which required me to grind for almost two hours to get past a boss; and second, the user interface was really poor and that complicated switching souls quickly to navigate around the map. Both of these were fixed in the next entry; in particular the Doppelganger soul allowed you to have two equipment sets readied.

I will definitely play this again as the secret character, so maybe I’ll report back then.
