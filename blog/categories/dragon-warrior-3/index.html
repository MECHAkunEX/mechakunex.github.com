Game: Dragon Warrior 3 (NES, 1988) (5)

Jan 28 2012
I played through Dragon Warrior 3 over the past week and a half. My opinions on this game are very similar to DW1 and DW2. This much more mechanically strong that DW2 but not as much of a leap as DW2 was over DW1. I enjoyed the class changing system. I used anti-grinding Game Genie codes to limit the amount I had to do it---one gave me more XP and the other more GP. The combat system gets pretty old, especially with random monsters. Fighting the bosses is typically really fun. I also played with a guide, so I didn't have to figure out where I needed to go myself, but I got the impression that it was much less obscure than DW2, but not as clear as even a SNES-era game. For these two reasons, I definitely didn't get a true DW3 experience, although I'm willing to bet that I wouldn't want that experience. Instead, I just enjoyed the story. The connection to the other games is easily the coolest part of this and really comes out of nowhere, but makes complete sense after the fact. I thought it was also really cool how you run into your father right at the end. It's really neat. I'm looking forward to the first truly played DW game.
