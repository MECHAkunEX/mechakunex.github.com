Book: The Complete Chronicles of Narnia, by C. S. Lewis (2000)20050817
<p>What I enjoyed most about the stories was the morality. It is
not too subtle and not too preachy, but is best called
'appropriate.' When Aslan rebukes the children and the characters
it makes sense and the lessons are real lessons; even the heroes of
a story are not morally pure.</p>

<p>While the books are good, it is possible to make too many
comparisons between Lewis and Tolkien. <cite>Narnia</cite> is
definitely loosely connected tales with a greater purpose only
Aslan understands, whereas <cite>The Lord of the Rings</cite> is a
concise epic that is complete story of its time, although it exists
atop a greater world.</p>
