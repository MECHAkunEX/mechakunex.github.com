Book: Capital in the Twenty-First Century, by Thomas Piketty (2014) (2)20140806
I'm in the process of reading the Introduction and I find the
author extremely aggravating. His focus on data and the way he
writes about it betrays a sense that there is no explanation for
capital accumulation and that there are no principles at work. The
way he lovingly writes about Marx is upsetting too.

I am also annoyed that he raises "income inequality" as a dangerous
specter without any explanations of why it is a bad thing. But
that's just another way of saying that I am deontologist and he is
a consequentialist.

When he discusses the Kuznets Curve, he employs ad-hominem attack
on the way the paper was given rather than arguing with its
content. :(

After reading the Introduction, I don't think I will dig into the
details. I feel a little bad to ignore something that offends my
sensibilities, but apart from the conclusions, the details seem to
be minutely tailored for experts in the field and I only have so
much time in life.
