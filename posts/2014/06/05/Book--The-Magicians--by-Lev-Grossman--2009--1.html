Book: The Magicians, by Lev Grossman (2009) (2)20140605
It is basically a realistic version of Harry Potter: what is life
really like for these wizards? Especially if they are loners that
start around when they would go to college and if after they left
they were all powerful with nothing to do. Unlike Harry Potter,
about 40% of the first book covers their entire time at school and
the real drama of the book happens when they leave school. That
said, the epic conclusion to their school experience is so awesome.

There are so many cools parts of the book...
- I love the very first scene of magic use.
- I love that Quentin is a normal guy who describes girls he meets
first based on their breasts. It's low class, of course, but it
is a great reflection of reality.
- I love the way part of the test is for him to make a fake world
with all sorts of weird details: translate a poem into it, then
translate it back, letting the weird grammatical problems and
word changes leak through. What a crazy idea!
- It's cool that we share the world with the characters: they
mention Star Wars and even Harry Potter. Unlike HP where the
world is totally different but superficially similar.
