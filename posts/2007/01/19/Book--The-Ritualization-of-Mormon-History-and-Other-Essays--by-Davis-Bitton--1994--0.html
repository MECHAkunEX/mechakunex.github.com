Book: The Ritualization of Mormon History and Other Essays, by Davis Bitton (1994)20070119
<p><cite>The
Ritualization of *REDACTED* History and Other
Essays</cite>, by Davis Bitton, is a collection of
essays about topics in *REDACTED* History that get little
attention. These are not, however, particularly
controversial topics.</p>

<p>The topics are: "The Saints as Human Beings", "Polygamy
Defended", "Growing Up on the *REDACTED* Frontier", "Bard of
*REDACTED*'s Dixie", "*REDACTED*ism's Encounted with Spiritualism",
"Dancing among the *REDACTED*s", "The Ordeal of Brigham Young,
Jr.", "The Exclusion of B. H. Roberts from Congress", and
"The Ritualization of *REDACTED* History."</p>

<p>The first three and the last three are by far the best
of the set.</p>

<p>The first has---among many other things---an
interesting record of early Saints who moved around
much <em>much</em> more than other Americans. (p. 3) And,
a really funny account of how a guy found his second
wife. (By solicitation at a factory, p. 9) It also
documents how polygamy was seen as a burden of membership
by many. (p. 10)</p>

<p>On the dancing:</p>

<blockquote>
<p>George Q. Cannon wrote that just as Adam and
Eve could be happy while having all the fruits but
one, so "round dancing is not absolutely necessary
to enjoyment." [p. 107]</p>
