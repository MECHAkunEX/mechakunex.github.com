Book: Naming and Necessity, by Saul A. Kripke (1980)20070821
<p>This books discusses the meaning of names and how
understanding this is influenced by counter-factual
reasoning. It is less `exciting' than it would be if I had
previously had an opinion on this matter. Given that I did
not, I had no horse in the race, so to speak.</p>

<p>A basic part of the argument is that speakers may not know the
"properties" of the referent of the name, nor may the
properties they do know be correct. Further, in these cases
the properties may be used to fix a reference, rather than
create a synonym.</p>
