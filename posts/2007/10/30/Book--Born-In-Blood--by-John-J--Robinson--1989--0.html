Book: Born In Blood, by John J. Robinson (1989)20071030
<p>This book defends the thesis that the Knights Templar that were not on continental Europe at the time their order was persecuted formed a mutual protection organization that evolved into Freemasonry. He makes an interesting case, but wastes much of the reader's energy repeating the same stories, claims, and evidence; additionally, he is recounts many events that have no bearing what-so-ever on his main story.</p>

<p>Among other things, he reports the rituals of Freemasons, trying to find the oldest versions he can. I can report that aside from a few tiny, superficial resemblances, it is nothing at all like the LDS temple ceremony, as is widely reported.</p>

<p>Some parts of this book are slightly interesting, but I cannot recommend it in general.</p></div>
