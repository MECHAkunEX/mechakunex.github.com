<p><strong>The *REDACTED* Trinity and other Trinities, by Stephen
T. Davis</strong></p>

<p>An account of how *REDACTED*s understand the Trinity, expressed
in traditional theological terms.</p>

<p><strong>Unity of Action and the Unity of God, by Benjamin
Huff</strong></p>

<p>An explanation about what it means in the scriptures when
it says that the Godhead "are one", yet are also separate
and distinct. The basic idea is that if we understand
"persons" as the originators of actions, then if the
Godhead were perfectly unanimous in their actions, then
they could be considered one person, while being separate
personages, or beings.</p>

<p><strong>Lockean Persons and LDS Metaphysics, by Jan-Erik
Jones</strong></p>

<p>A discussion of whether in LDS theology the soul is
materalistic with a connection drawn to Lockean
metaphysics.</p>

<p><strong>Latter-day Saint Conscience, by Rosalynde
F. Welch</strong></p>

<p>An analysis of what Latter-day Saints say in place of the
term "conscience", as used by other religious movements,
with connections to history and discourse.</p>

<p><strong>Embodied Knowledge of God, by Jennifer
C. Lane</strong></p>

<p>An interesting take on how rituals and acting like Christ
give us knowledge of God that is deeper than we would have
if there were to be new sections added to the Doctrine
&amp; Covenants regularly.</p>
