Book: The Portrait of a Lady, by Henry James (1881)20080312
<p>A very good read. The characters are incredibly vivid and their growth is remarkable. Comparing Isabel in the beginning of the book to the end is quite unbelievable if you were not with her throughout. My favourite character is certainly Pansy: her final scene with Isabel changes her from a two-dimensional caricature to a living animal.</p>

<p>Some charming quotes from the start of the book:</p>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>"My excuse for not rowing is that my cousin [Isabel] rows so well," said Ralph. "She does everything well. She touches nothing that she doesn't adorn!"</p>
<p>"It makes one want to be touched, Miss Archer," Lord Warburton declared. [p. 61]</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>"I always want to know the things one shouldn't do," [said Isabel.]</p>
<p>"So as to do them?" asked her aunt.</p>
<p>"So as to choose," said Isabel. [p. 63]</p>
</blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>"I call people rich when they're able to meet the requirements of their imagination." [Ralph Touchett, p. 173]</p>
</blockquote>
