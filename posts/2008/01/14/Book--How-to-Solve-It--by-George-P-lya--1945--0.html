Book: How to Solve It, by George Pólya (1945)20080114
<p>I highly recommend this book. It teaches the <a
href="http://htdp.org/"><cite>How to Design Programs</cite></a>
Design Recipe for all of problem solving, with extensive commentary
on the various ways approaching problems and understanding
solutions once you've got them.</p>

<p>The following quip is therein: "[A traditional mathematics
professor] writes <em>a</em>, he says <em>b</em>, he means
<em>c</em>; but it should be <em>d</em>" (p. 208)</p></div>
