Book: A Marginal Jew: Rethinking the Historical Jesus: Companions and Competitors, Vol 3., by John P. Meier (2001)20080211
<p>This book covers the people who associated with Jesus (crowds,
disciples, and the Twelve) and those who were in competition with
him for followers (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, etc). Some
interesting points: Meier doesn't think "apostles" are the same as
"the Twelve" (1 Cor. 15:3-7 is a prize) and is very vigilant
against Church tradition to the contrary, as well as identifying
various people with those of similar (or anonymous) nomenclature;
and there is surprisingly little information available about the
Pharisees and the Sadducees.</p>

<p>This book was much more tedious than the last. These are
monumental tomes that don't leave any rock unturned. Not for
someone who just wants to scratch the surface.</p></div>
