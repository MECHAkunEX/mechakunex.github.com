Book: The Best Poems of the English Language: From Chaucer to Robert Frost, edited by Harold Bloom (2004)20101212
Since June 2007, I've read a poem (or section of a poem) from
this book every day. Today, I finished the last poem. It's been
very nice to get acquainted with more authors and poetry in
general. Bloom's commentary is also always very interesting.

In the beginning, I wrote down a few notes, but looking back at the
page numbers, I have no idea what I thought was interesting about
some of these lines two years ago.

Such an expansive taste of poetry has solidified my opinions
about what I like and what I don't: I really like poetry that
<em>sounds</em> good when you read it. I also really like poetry
that is very vivid. I don't care much for abstract or navel-gazing
in poetry. Of Bloom's selections, most of his new poetry is stuff I
don't care for and most of his older stuff, I do like.

I'm glad I read this, but I will be taking a little break from
poetry for a bit. When I come back, I think I will read all of
Shakespeare's work.
