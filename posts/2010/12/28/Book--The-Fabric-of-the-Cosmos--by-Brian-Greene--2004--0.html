Book: The Fabric of the Cosmos, by Brian Greene (2004)20101228
For the past month or so, I've been reading The Fabric of the
Cosmos. I read The Elegant Universe when I was in college, so I was
interested in reading this book to catch up on new developments and
refresh myself on old from a different perspective.

It is fun to read an think about physics and imagine how *REDACTED*
theology can work into many of the theories Greene discusses. It's
fun to hear about all the esoteric experiments imagined and
performed to confirm and test this stuff. Still, I don't think I'd
want to be a physicist, but I am happy to look in from the outside
and try to learn something.
