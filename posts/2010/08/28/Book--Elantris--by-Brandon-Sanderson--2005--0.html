Book: Elantris, by Brandon Sanderson (2005)20100828
One of my graduate students recommended this to me and I read it
after I finished the Scott Pilgrim books.

I don't normally read fantasy books and I don't normally get very
sucked into a book, but this was quite different. It's hard to
place exactly, but I think I like this book because it does such a
good job of defying your expectations of the genre. The kinds of
characters that are involved is very unique (including a formidable
female) and the world is definitely fascinating.

If I had to compare this to another book, I'd compare it to the
first Dune book where we have political intrigue, some religious
fervor, a wasteland-like habitat, etc. Since I like Dune so much,
this was just right for me.

I'm also very happy that there is no sequel to this book. I find
it very aggravating that so many fantasy authors seem to not be
able to control themselves and bring a story to completion.

The closest thing I could find to not liking it is that near the
end their are a few reveals that insinuate that they were
foreshadowed, when in my opinion, they were not and read more like
Deus Ex Machina. Even so, I'm very impressed by the level of
foreshadowing and universe building where the more dramatic moments
are believable.
