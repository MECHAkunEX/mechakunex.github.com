Game: Shadow of the Colossus (PS2, 2005) (3)20130705
I took an attempt at the ninth colossi over an hour. Man, it was
painful. I could tell pretty quickly what I was supposed to do:
get it to walk over the geyser, shoot its feet, and then climb on
it. In the hour I played, I got him to go over the geyser maybe 4
times and only got to climb on it twice, and never actually got on
its back. It was so painful and it made me not want to play the
game anymore.

Overall, I really like the feel of this game, but I find it too
frustrating. This boss is a great example of why: the game is very
committed to creating a "real" world with "real" beasts that you
have to take down. This commitment to reality is inspiring and
pretty neat, but it can cause great annoyances: a very long trip
to every boss fight, waiting while the animals swim or fly around
in great circles, etc. I feel like they should've been programmed
to "cooperate" more with the player's goals.

Given my scarce time, particularly at the place where I played it,
it wasn't worth fighting through.
