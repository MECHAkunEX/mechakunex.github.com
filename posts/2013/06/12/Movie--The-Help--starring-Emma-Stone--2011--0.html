Movie: The Help, starring Emma Stone (2011)20130612
This was a well-played movie. The acting was very good and the
story had lots of fun moments.

The overall plot and the interpretation the film seems to want you
to take away has some annoyances though. For example, a white
women saves the black people, so it isn't really a movie about
black empowerment. I dislike that Hilly isn't just racist, but
she's evil in general, so we can't isolate why she's bad. 

I like that the conclusion is that nothing has changed, because
Hilly/Elizabeth/Aibileen mirror the
president/*REDACTED*lotte/Constantine. (I like this, because I like
tragedy and I don't think such a book would really be effective.)
Similarly, I like that Stuart is ultimately a punk, although he's
such a minor character, it almost doesn't matter. I like the
relationship between Minny and Celia, because Minny is teaching
Celia how to enforce racism; it's like "raunch culture" and
/Female Chauvinist Pigs/.
