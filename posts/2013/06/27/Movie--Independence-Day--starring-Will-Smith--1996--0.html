Movie: Independence Day, starring Will Smith (1996)20130627
Yet another legacy movie that was very powerful when it came out. I
enjoyed this a lot more than I thought I would on a rewatch. There
really aren't any bad early special effects to bring it down.

The story is very cool and I like how there's never really any
explanation for the aliens. Although the President "sees one's
thoughts" and says they are "like locusts, they come to a planet,
use all its resources and move on", there's no more. It's funny
that this sounds bad and ominous but in reality that's really all
you would expect us to do in the long run to each planet. They can
only support so much activity. You can either send the "excess"
people on to the next planet and have many small civilizations in
equilibrium or keep everyone together in a moving colony like that
depicted. I see no reason to obviously prefer one over the other.

I was surprised and pleased that Will Smith's one liners still
worked. I was very moved by the President's speech. Overall, it was
a great movie, again.
