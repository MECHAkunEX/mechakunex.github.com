Book: Apollo's Angels, by Jennifer Homans (2010) (11)20130111
When I was reading today about ballet in post-WWII Britain, I came
across the strange discussion of /The Invitation/, a 1960 ballet by
the Royal Ballet Touring Company that featured a re-enacted rape
scene on stage. There's a footnote about how ballet was technically
not under the purview of the censors. As an example:

"In August 1960, representatives from the Lord Chamberlin's
office attended a performance of /Les Ballets Africains/ to
determine if the production was ballet or theatre. If ballet,
bare breasts were permissible; if theatre, strictly
forbidden. The authorities agreed it was a ballet---and the
press had a field day with photos." (pg. 433)

How strange.
