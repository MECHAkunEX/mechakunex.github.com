Game: Shining in the Darkness (GEN, 1991)20130803
I'd heard of this game for a long time but didn't really know
anything about it. It's basically a Japanese first-person dungeon
crawler. The anime scene introducing the story was cute, as is the
interface of walking around the town and talking to people. Once
you're in the dungeon... it is a first-person dungeon crawl with
no map.

It's clear how you would beat the game: build your own map and
meticulously track where you are in it. I am way too old to do
that. Maybe if I wrote a program to build the map for me, I would
consider it, but I can't imagine actually having a piece of graph
paper in front of me. The biggest pain is not just having the map
but keeping track of where you are on it while you're playing. I
glanced at a map of the first level online but realized it was
useless.

The shop interface has the classic problem of not knowing if an
item is better and not allowing you to equip it. What a pain.

The combat is very straight forward with only one choice at any
time. I expect that you get more people and can cast spells, but
it doesn't feel like the combat will be very compelling.

Overall, that means it's a drop.
