Game: Devil May Cry 3: Special Edition (HD Collection) (PS3, 2012) (5)20130801
I played through the rest of the game tonight. It was basically
just two boss fights. Since I had never used any Gold Skulls or
any healing items the entire game, it was easy to just use them
and push through the boss fights, although they were still a
little hard. I liked fighting during the Credits roll. I was also
impressed with the large number of unlocks that I got at the end
of the game.

Overall, I found the game enjoyable but not especially amazing. I
can imagine having loved it when it came out and wanting to play
through every possible mode, like I did with Bayonetta, but I
think it is out-classed by Bayonetta generally, so I don't think
I'll do that now. In particular, I found that I was able to limit
myself to a pretty simple strategy and make it through the whole
game---whereas in Bayo I had to modify my strategy as I got
further along. 

However, the game definitely got harder, but it was more about
complicated boss patterns, etc, rather than asking for you to
change your core skills. As a simple example, I stayed with the
starting weapons and style the entire game without much need for
change---just a few instances of using a better weapon for a
particular weakness.
