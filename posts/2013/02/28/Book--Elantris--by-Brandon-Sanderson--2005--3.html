Book: Elantris, by Brandon Sanderson (2005) (4)20130228
I read seven chapters today.

Compare: "Hrathen would ever bear the scars of what he had done,
but it was better that one man suffer than an entire nation
continue in heresy." (Elantris, p203) vs "Behold the Lord slayeth
the wicked to bring forth his righteous purposes. It is better that
one man should perish than that a nation should dwindle and perish
in unbelief." (1 Nephi 4:13)

Given that I know what will happen in the end, it is exciting to
see the foreshadowing (and foreboding) about the deadly monks of
the East. It's very cool.

Another way to look at what Raoden is doing in Elantris: Since he
giving everyone something to do and their lack of anything to do
was making them miserable, in a sense he is saying that if you (a)
live forever, (b) don't change/grow, and (c) don't have anything to
do, then you will be miserable. This is interesting from a *REDACTED*
perspective, because that describes perfectly what non-*REDACTED*
Heaven is like, whereas in the *REDACTED* concept of Heaven, everyone
has purpose and growth, and thus, according to /Elantris/,
happiness.

I really enjoyed how Raoden "converted" Taan with love and
reminding him of the beauty of the world.
