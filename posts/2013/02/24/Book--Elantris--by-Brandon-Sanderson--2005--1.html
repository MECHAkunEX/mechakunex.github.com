Book: Elantris, by Brandon Sanderson (2005) (2)20130224
I read quite a few more chapters today and having a smattering of
thoughts.

- The contrast between Dilaf's zealotry and Hrathen's practicality
is very interesting. For example, Dilaf says something like
"Nothing can stand in Jaddeth's way" as an argument against why
they should do some plan for the Kae. Hrathen's response is,
"Then why isn't the whole world converted?" One idea I can see in
this is that often the "sophisticated theology" of the
upper-class of religionists make them have almost totally
different beliefs than the lower-class, to the point that the
upper-class may not even seem like they believe at all. I get
this impression about *REDACTED*ism a lot when people talk about how
to treat new converts, especially those in far away places.

- Hrathen gives a very *REDACTED* explanation of the Svarkiss: "Nothing
is more bitter than a soul who had had its chance and thrown it
away." This is the way that Satan and his followers are discussed
a lot in *REDACTED*ism and how many ex-members are seen.

- A poignant moment in one of Raoden's chapters is the discussion
of how Elantris was not built for normal people to live
in---there are no wells or furnances or lights---and that after
the fall of AonDor it is totally desolate. How is this related to
Modern worry about a too heavy reliance on technology and the
desire to train ourselves to live without in times of disaster?

- I like that Sarene's plan to change the government is so
libertarian and capitalistic. You can almost see the debate on
what to do as a debate about how to intervene in communist
countries and/or get them to change for the better. Freedom
works.
