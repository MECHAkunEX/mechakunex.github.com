Book: The Enigma of Japanese Power, by Karel van Wolferen (1989)20110408
I've been reading this very interesting book of the past month or
so. The summary on Wikipedia is pretty accurate.

From one perspective, I'd summarize his point as: Japan does not
have a central government that can make decisions, instead it has
many competing political powers. This means there is no unified
direction and this is bad for the Japanese because good things for
the people can't be done and bad for foreigners because
negotiations can't happen, because there is no one to negotiate
with.

As an unabashed anarchist, I like the idea of incompetent
government. However, Wolferen makes a good case that the many
competing powers do a lot of harm individually to the Japanese
people and there's no one to stop them. This book makes me want to
live in Japan less.

As an aside, some of the things that he discusses as particularly
bad---such as the behavior of police---seem really good to me.

I'd recommend skimming the first half of the book to anyone
interested in Japan. The last half is very esoteric and focused on
too many details of politics and business, in my opinion.
