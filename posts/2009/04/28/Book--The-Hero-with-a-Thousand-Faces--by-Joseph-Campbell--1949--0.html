Book: The Hero with a Thousand Faces, by Joseph Campbell (1949)20090428
<p>This book is much less interesting than I thought it would
be. Every chapter is a brief summary of many myths related to the
idea. Most of the connection and larger arc of these myths is
actually left to the reader to infer. You can certainly infer
something, but it is definitely not as profound as you often hear
that this book is. Verdict: A decent summary of interesting
commonalities in myth, but I could leave it.</p>

<p><em>The massive amount of Freudian psychology the book
references is amazing. It reminds me of one of my favourite quotes:
"Psychoanalysis is itself the illness of which it purports to be
the cure." - Karl Kraus</em></p></div>
