Book: God Emperor of Dune, by Frank Herbert (1981)20060129
<p>I very much enjoy the idea of incredibly long-term plans. Leto's plans can take place of thousands of years, because of his enhanced life-span. The idea about managing the breeding of his descendents is very curious.</p>

<p>This book also contains a great deal of interesting musing about the difference between men and women, particularly with regards to woman-dominated societies and women-in-war. This and the discussion of ancestral memory and the amount of history living in everyone is very... thought provoking.</p>

<p>Quotes:</p>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>"A bad administrator s more concerned with reports than with decisions. He wants the hard record which he can display as an excuse for his errors."</p>
<p>"And good administrators?"</p>
<p>"Oh, they depend on verbal orders. They never lie about what they've done if their verbal orders cause problems, and they surround themselves with people able to act wisely on the basis of verbal orders. Often, the most important piece of information is that something has gone wrong. Bad administrators hide their mistakes until it's too late to make corrections." [p. 241]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>At a completely different moment of the story:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>She said: "My Uncle Malky used to say that love was a bad bargain because you get no guarantees."</p>
<p>"Your Uncle Malky was a wise man."</p>
<p>"He was stupid! Love <em>needs</em> no guarantees."</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
