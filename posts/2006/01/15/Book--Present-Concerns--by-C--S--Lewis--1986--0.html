Book: Present Concerns, by C. S. Lewis (1986)20060115
<p>From <cite>Equality</cite>:</p>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>I am a democrat because I believe in the Fall of Man. I think most people are democrats for the opposite reason. A great deal of democratic enthusiasm descends from te ideas of people like Rousseau, who believed in democracy because they thought mankind so wise and good that everyone deserves a share in the government. The danger of defending democracy on those grounds is that they're not true. And whenever their weakness is exposed, the people who prefer tyranny make capital out of the exposure. [...] The real reason for democracy is just the reverse. Mankind is so fallen that no man can be trusted with unchecked power over his fellows. Aristotle said that some people were only fit to be slaves. I do not contradict him. But I reject slavery because I see no men fit to be masters. [p. 17]</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p>In <cite>Modern Man and his Categories of Thought</cite>, he says that "in our student population [there is] a lowering of metaphysical energy" (p. 63), because there is too much mixed company of males and females; with the men always showing off and the females uninterested in metaphysics.</p>

<p><cite>On Living in an Atomic Age</cite> is amazing. <a href='http://stuartbuck.blogspot.com/2002/12/i-came-across-old-c.html'>Stuart Buck</a> has a nice quote.</p>
