Book: The Autobiography of Benjamin Franklin, by Benjamin Franklin (1791)20060716
<p>This is a very odd book. Many of the events that you may expect to read about are not mentioned at all. There is very little about the revolution and Franklin's role in it. Regardless, it was fairly interesting.</p>

<p>The most interesting thing for me were the sections on the `Plan' which is mentioned throughout, but primarily discussed on page 66:</p>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>Perhaps the most important part of that journal is the <em>plan</em> to be found in it, which I formed at sea, for reglating my future conduct in life. It is the more remarkable, as being formed when I was so young, and yet being pretty faithfully adhered to quite thro' to old age. [p. 38]</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>

<p>Advice on virtue:</p>
<blockquote>
<blockquote>
<p>In reality, there is, perhaps, no one of our natural passions so hard to subdue as <em>pride</em>. Disguise it, struggle with it, beat it down, stifle it, mortify it as much as one pleases, it is still alive, and will every now and then peep out and show itself; you will see it, perhaps, often in this history; for, even if I could conceive that I had compleatly overcome it, I should probably be proud of my humility. [p. 72]</p>
</blockquote>
</blockquote>
