Book: The Grand Fundamental Principles of Mormonism: Joseph Smith's Unfinished Reformation, by Don Bradley (2006)20060827
<p>This article talks about a series of sermons in the last year of
Joseph Smith's life about the principles of *REDACTED*ism: truth,
friendship, and relief.</p>
<blockquote>
<p>Prior to declaring these "grand fundamental principles," Joseph
had attempted to define *REDACTED*ism in <em>doctrinal</em> terms in
D&amp;C 20, the Lectures of Faith, and the Articles of Faith. But
each of these inevitably failed to provide a timeless or final
definition of the faith's essence. The task of capturing *REDACTED*ism
in a creedal statement was Sisyphean, because *REDACTED*ism was more
committed to the <em>principle</em> of forever acquiring truth
than to any particular formulation of the truth. It was a circle
no state set of doctrines could square. [p. 36]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The impact of these principles of Christian unity:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>But Joseph averred that he would not use his authority to press
others to follow his beliefs and revelations, nor would he condemn
them for failing to do so: "If I esteem mankind to be in error
shall I bear them down? No! I will lift them up &amp; [each] in
his own way if I cannot persuade him my way is better! ... I will
ask no man to believe as I do." [p. 37-38]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>On relief:</p>
<blockquote>
<p>As R. Dennis Potter has observed, "For King Benjamin, the
fundamental sin... is the failure to take care of the poor." Since
for Benjamin, to serve others was to serve God, to neglect others
in need was to neglect God, jeopardizing one's standing before
him. In Benjamin's theology, receiving a remission of sins
requires faith and repentence, but "from day to day" requires that
one "impart of [one's] substance to the poor" (Mosiah
4:26). [p. 38]</p>
</blockquote>
<p>I think this can be interpreted too literally. We must also
consider the need to provide relief and comfort for those who are
spiritually poor, i.e. lacking in Christ-like qualities. The Saints
must be perfected and we are on the same team, working for the same
goal.</p>
</blockquote>
