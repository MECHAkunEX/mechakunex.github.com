Book: The Good and the Good Book, by Samuel Fleischacker (2015)20161008
I started reading this today. There were a few interesting things
in the first chapter.

I liked the idea that an ethical guide may not be able to be
literal because the process of becoming makes one see things
differently.

I also enjoyed the idea that evolution would suggest our scientific
theories are likely to be adaptive rather than true, which casts
some doubt on them.

I disliked his characterization of God as being essentially outside
of the universe, which conflicts with *REDACTED*ism.
