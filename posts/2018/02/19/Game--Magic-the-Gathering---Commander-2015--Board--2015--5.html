Game: Magic the Gathering - Commander 2015 (Board, 2015) (6)20180219
I did a bunch of reading about how to play MtG in a solitaire
style. [This site](http://www.jmgariepy.com/2014/06/19/seven-solitaire-formats-for-magic-the-gathering/) had a bunch of different options.

[Aaron's Solitaire](http://www.angelfire.com/games4/magicvariants/Aarons_Solitaire.htm) seems the best because basically the opponent
always uses whatever is next on their deck, which gives them an
early game advantage. They then follow an automated attack pattern
where they always attack with everything and block with everything
then match high defense with low attack. Since they can cast
anything immediately, they have a big advantage.

[Deep IQ](https://magic.wizards.com/en/articles/archive/serious-fun/deep-iq-2013-06-11) (extended) and ([original](http://www.angelfire.com/games4/magicvariants/Deep_IQ.htm)) is a more complicated system
that doesn't actually use an opponent deck. Instead, the enemy
does random things from a table that starts weak and then becomes
more powerful. The main idea is that they have a whole bunch of
tokens and they have random powers.
