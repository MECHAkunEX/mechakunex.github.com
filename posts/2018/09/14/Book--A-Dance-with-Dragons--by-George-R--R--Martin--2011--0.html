Book: A Dance with Dragons, by George R. R. Martin (2011) - Prologue & Chapter 120180914
The perspective of the Warg was really interesting. I like the wolf
thinking about the "claws that men carried" and how the Wildlings
save the attack on the Wall. It's cool that the woodswitch "saw"
the ships coming for them. His whole backstory was really
interesting... experiencing many deaths, being a villain for a
community, and so on. You can really see why the wargs would be
outcasts, rather than seen as awesome secret weapons by them. The
blue-eye shadows and the "children on the elk's back" were very
cool.

Tyrion's chapter was much more ambiguous about his future than the
corresponding thing in the show. The connection with Illyrio as
Varys' friend is cool, and it is neat that he really considers
going to Dorne or even the Wall! (Atoning for old sins or making
new ones!) It's amazing how much he thinks about Taisha. This
chapter has awesome quotes: Only thing worse than dwarf with no
nose is one without gold; Drowning yourself cup by cup wastes time;
best part of me for best part of her; etc. It was neat to hear the
Pentosi reflect on the use of banners by the Westerosi. I think the
book does a much better job at showing how devastating the war was
on the Seven Kingdoms and Illyrio really picks up that this will
make Dany look more like a savior than a conqueror.
