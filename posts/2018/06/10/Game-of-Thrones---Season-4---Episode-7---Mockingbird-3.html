Game of Thrones - Season 4 - Episode 7 - Mockingbird (4)20180610
Shae's reversal is the most unsupported one in the whole series,
I think. Arya killing that guy was so intense... she did it so
fast. I love Bronn's logic in this episode. "I like you, I just
like myself more." Dany's having him strip is very
uncharacteristic of her. I feel like they've implied that she was
charmed by him, but not recently.

Melisandre's discussion of tricking people into believing in God
is very interesting. It is crazy how Melisandre is already
planning to kill Shireen.

It's cool how Sansa first sees snow after so long and the Stark
theme plays. Littlefinger's explanation of killing Joffrey is
carefully chosen for Sansa to hear. It is very awkward how he
goes from saying she could be his daughter to kissing her. Lysa
is so touched.
