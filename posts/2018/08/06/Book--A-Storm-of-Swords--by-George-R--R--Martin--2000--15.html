Book: A Storm of Swords, by George R. R. Martin (2000) - Chapter 27 & 28 & 29 & 30 & 3120180806
I loved Dany's scene, although it is much more difficult to write a
scene like the climax than to show it visually.

It was interesting that Sansa's wedding was told from her
perspective, as I felt like the corresponding scene in the show was
more focused on Tyrion. It is sad that she had no idea right up
until the end.

It was funny how Arya thought that the girl who claimed to be the
king's daughter looked like Gendry. Hah! The cliffhanger about it
being the Hound is interesting. I wonder if they'll actually
connect up with Thoros.

It is awesome how Jon is slowly understanding and becoming familiar
with the Free Folk and their culture. The scaling of the Wall was
much less dangerous for him and the scene of grandeur once they got
up was less.

Jaime's pride and attitude are surprisingly endearing for someone
so otherwise terrible. I like the subtle clues about the
Fray/Bolton alliance from this chapter.
