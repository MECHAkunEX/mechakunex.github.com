Book: A Feast for Crows, by George R. R. Martin (2005) - Chapter 3 & 4 & 5 & 6 & 7 & 820180829
Cersei is so proud and paranoid. It is quite ridiculous.

As crazy as Cersei is, Brienne is so serious and unflinching. I'm
sad that she had no Podrick to take care of and bounce off of.

It is strange to only see Jon from afar with Sam's perspective. I
really enjoyed the way that Jon commanded him. I feel like there is
much less love between Sam & Gilly.

Arya's story is very fascinating, because she is becoming "No One"
with no name and she has gone through so many different names on
her journey. I love that she's hungry for vengeance.

Cersei's ambition to be remembered gloriously is very well
defined. I thought that Kevan's advice was very good and his
reasons for being against her were interesting. It is much harder
for Cersei to assert her power in the book, or at least we get to
observe it happening.

Jaime's regret for his actions and disgust at Cersei's actions is
inspiring.
