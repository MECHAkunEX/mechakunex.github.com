Game: A Single Moment (Board, 2016)20180310
I wrote down a bit of elaboration for the romantic comedy rules to
better understand the structure of the game. My wife didn't want
to play though. Bummer.


Virtues:
1. First Sight
2. Friends
3. Differences
4. Secret Discovery
5. Trust
6. Conflict
7. Recognition

Steps:
1. Opening Scene
a. P1: Who am I
b. P2: Who am I
c. P1: Time/period
d. P2: Location
e. P1: Length of time for narrative
f. P2: Main virtue of story
This defines all the other chapters, sometimes as a starting
point.
2. P1: Chapter 1 --- The Setup
a. P1: Draw a virtue (draw 2, choose 1)
b. P1: Declare focus of chapter
c. P1: Set the scene (and key role)
d. Play through
- 7 choice tokens to go around
- Every time a player wins a ruling, the other player
gets a choice.
- The active player always gets to decide.
e. Chapter climax
- Each choice becomes a die
- May add dice (up to 7) but costs a Heartbreak
- Whoever exemplified virtue gets 2 more.
- Roll dice, highest total wins.
- Victor gets Throbs equal to Choices and narrates ending
- Losers gets Nemesis equal to Victor's Throbs plus one for
every die above 3.
- On a tie, both get Throbs (=own) & Nemesis (=opponent),
plus active gets a Heartbreak
f. Resolve chapter          
3. P2: Chapter 2 --- Meet Cute
4. P1: Chapter 3 --- The First Date
5. P2: Chapter 4 --- The Honey Moon
6. P1: Chapter 5 --- The Fight
7. P2: Chapter 6 --- Time Apart
8. Finale --- The Resolution
a. Deal with Nemesis
- Channel: Roll 1 die per Nemesis, even become Throbs, odds
become Heartbreak
- Let Go: Nemesis must be >10, discard all, plus remove N/2
Heartbreak.
b. Most Nemesis goes first as "Attacker"
c. Series of "Exchanges"
- Attacker spends a Throb for 1 die
- Defender gets a die for free.
- Each can spend a Throb to get extra die (up to 3 die total)
- Attacker must get more than Defender
- Winner describes resolution
- Losers gets a Heartbreak
- Tie causes blind Throb bid, if you lose get a Heartbreak
d. Killing Strike
- Once one player is out of Throbs, the other's Throbs become
Heartbreaks if the Defender loses.
- If the Attacker wins, then they win (more severely if the
Defender has more Heartbreak)
- If the Attacker fails, then the Defender gets to make a
killing strike.
- If still loses, the one with the least Heartbreak wins.
9. Epilogue
- Triumphant narrates ending
